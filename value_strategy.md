# Value Stock Selection Strategy

**Goal**: Pick 3 undervalued stocks by end of year, hold 1–3 years.

---

## The 3-Filter Funnel

### Filter 1 — Valuation (is it cheap?)
- P/E ratio < 15 (market average ~20)
- P/B ratio < 1.5
- P/FCF (price-to-free-cash-flow) < 15

### Filter 2 — Quality (is the business healthy?)
- Debt/Equity < 0.5
- ROE > 10% consistently for 3+ years
- Positive free cash flow every year for 3+ years

### Filter 3 — Moat (can it stay good?)
- Simple business you can explain in one sentence
- Dominant in a niche, or has strong brand/switching costs
- Revenue growing or stable — not shrinking

---

## Where to Screen

Use **finviz.com/screener** (free) with these settings:
- P/E: Under 15
- P/B: Under 1.5
- Debt/Eq: Under 0.5
- ROE: Over 10%
- Country: USA

This gives ~50–100 candidates. Then manually check FCF and moat for the top 10.

---

## Selection Process

1. Run the screen → get ~50 names
2. Eliminate anything you don't understand
3. For remaining, check 3-year FCF trend on Macrotrends.net
4. Pick top 3 with the lowest P/FCF + strongest FCF trend

---

## Position Sizing

- Equal weight: 33% each
- Set a mental stop: if thesis breaks (e.g., company takes on massive debt), sell
- Review quarterly — don't check daily

---

## Classic Value Sectors to Focus On

Cheap stocks tend to cluster in: **financials, energy, industrials, healthcare**.
Avoid speculative tech with no earnings.

## Notes

- This is Graham-style value investing — simple, low-churn, no daily monitoring needed.
- Run the screen once a month, update your shortlist, finalize 3 picks by December.
- Data sources: finviz.com (screening), macrotrends.net (FCF history), SEC EDGAR (10-K filings)
